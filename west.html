Great Gardens of the West

Butterflies Native to the Western part of the United States include:

Phyciodes mylitta Mylitta Crescent

  • Phyciodes mylitta Mylitta Crescents lay their eggs under the leave of plants.
  • Some Phyciodes mylitta Mylitta Crescents live in high mountains.
  • Travel from Canada to Mexico.

Polygonia satyrus Satyr Comma

  • Eggs are planted on the leaves of nettle plants.
  • Caterpillars create silk, which is used to create a shelter with the leaves. /li>
  • Polygonia satyrus Satyr Commas will hibernate.
  • Polygonia satyrus Satyr Commas are found from Canada to Baja California.

Vanessa annabella West Coast Lady

  • Vanessa annabella West Coast Ladys lay eggs on the topside of the leaves
  • Caterpillars create silk, which is used to create a shelter with the leaves.
  • Polygonia satyrus Satyr Commas will hibernate.
