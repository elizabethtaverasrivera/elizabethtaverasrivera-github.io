Great Gardens of the North

Butterflies Native to the Northern part of the United States include:

Epargyreus clarus Silver-spotted Skipper

  • Caterpillars eat during the evening hours.
  • As the Epargyreus clarus Silver-spotted Skipper evolves its moves various times from one self-made shelter to another.
  • Eggs are laid on the topside of leaves.

Erynnis icelus Dreamy Duskywing

  • Enjoys cool, moist areas.
  • Oftentimes, brown.
  • Small body.

Atalopedes campestris Sachem

  • Oftentimes, yellow top and brown underbody.
  • Caterpillars grow within blades of grass.

Northern butterflies enjoy the following flowers/plants:

  • Butterfly Bush, milkweeds
  • Milkweeds
  • Wisteria
  • Alfalfa
  • Purple Coneflowers
