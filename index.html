Butterflies

Part of the insect family lepidoptera, butterflies bring color and magic to our world.

Like most insects, butterflies use their antenas to sense smells and they use their feet to taste.

While butterflies appear fragile they can live as long as one year.

Butterflies are found in most part of the world and there are over 25, 000 species.

One of the most impressive aspect of a butterfly's life is its ability to metamorphosize.

A Butterfly's Life Cycle

Butterflies develop through 4 stages.

Egg

  • Eggs are laid on leaves.
  • Eggs are minute.
  • After 5 days the egg becomes a caterpillar.

Caterpillar or Larvae

  • Caterpillars have between 6-10 pairs of legs.
  • They resemble worms.
  • Caterpillars feed on leaves and flowers.

Chrysalis or Pupa

  • In the chrysalis stage, butterflies feed on plants as well as aphids or small insects.
  • At this stage, the butterfly is done growing.
  • The final stage of the metamorphosis is completed during this stage.

Butterfly

  • Adult butterflies feed on nectar, tree sap and fruits.
  • Butterflies use their tongues to sip nectar from the flowers.

A World Without Butterflies

  • We need Butterflies as they provide a wide range of environmental benefits, including pollination and natural pest control.
  • Moths and butterflies are also an important element of the food chain and are prey for birds, bats and other insectivorous animals.
  • For example, in Britain and Ireland, Blue Tits eat an estimated 50 billion moth caterpillars each year.
  • Butterflies support a range of other predators and parasites. Many predators and parasites are dependant on specific butterfly species to survive.
