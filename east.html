Great Gardens of the East

Butterflies Native to the Eastern part of the United States include:

Epargyreus clarus Silver-spotted Skipper

  • Has silver and brown wings.
  • Epargyreus clarus Silver-spotted Skipper have cover under the leaves during the rain.
  • Epargyreus clarus Silver-spotted Skippers prefer red, purple and white flowers over yellow flowers.

Erynnis icelus Dreamy Duskywing

  • Erynnis icelus Dreamy Duskywings have black wings.
  • Travel from Nova Scotia to Georgia.
  • Erynnis icelus Dreamy Duskywings lay single eggs.

Atalopedes campestris Sachem

  • Atalopedes campestris Sachems are generally yellow, the males are more orange and the the females are more brown.
  • Females have a white spot.
  • Travel from Virginia to Brazil

Eastern butterflies enjoy the following flowers/plants:

  • Black locust
  • Honey locust
  • Willows
  • Poplars
  • Aspens
